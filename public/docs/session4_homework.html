Session 4 follow-up — due before Session 5
RUN Run the Engine on 2 More Topics

We built the research engine together on the call and ran it on one topic. Now put it to work on two more real questions for HouseAccount.

STEP 1 — WRITE THE TOPIC FILES
Create two new .md files in your topics/ directory. Each one should frame a research question that matters to your business right now. Some ideas:
  • Pricing intelligence — what are competitors charging for common handyman services in your active markets?
  • Provider retention — how do other home services platforms keep their best providers from leaving?
  • Fulfillment optimization — what do marketplace operators say about reducing time-to-match?
  • Market expansion — which adjacent service categories are growing fastest?
  • Customer acquisition — how do home services marketplaces acquire homeowners cost-effectively?
Pick whatever's most useful to you. The topic file should include: the specific question, why it matters to HouseAccount, what you already know, and any starting sources if you have them.
STEP 2 — RUN EACH ONE
Open Claude Code in your research engine directory. For each topic:
Research the topic in topics/[your-topic].md. Follow the methodology, extract using the schema, and produce a synthesis report in output/.
Save the output. Read it. Is it useful? Would you make a decision based on it?
WHAT TO NOTICE
Pay attention to what the engine does well and where it falls short. Are the sources relevant? Is the extraction schema capturing the right things? Is the synthesis at the right level of detail? Every observation is a potential improvement to the .md files.
IMPROVE Improve the System

After running the engine a few times, you'll notice patterns in what works and what doesn't. This is the iteration step — but you're iterating on the system, not individual outputs.

UPDATE THE .MD FILES
Based on your runs, improve at least one of these:
  • methodology.md — Are the right sources being prioritized? Should you add or remove source categories? Is the depth right?
  • extraction.md — Are there fields you want that aren't being captured? Fields that are always empty or useless? Adjust the schema.
  • synthesis.md — Is the report format useful? Too long? Too shallow? Missing a section you want?
RE-RUN AND COMPARE
After updating, re-run one of your topics and compare the new output to the original. Is it better? The .md files should be noticeably improved by the end of the week compared to what we built on the call.
This is the real skill. Anyone can ask Claude to research something. The operator advantage is building a system that gets better every time you use it. The .md files are your intellectual property — they encode your judgment about what matters and how to find it.
STRETCH Point It at Something Else

The engine should work on any topic, not just home services. Test that assumption.

PICK A DIFFERENT DOMAIN
Write a topic file for something unrelated to HouseAccount:
  • An investment thesis you're curious about
  • A technology trend (AI in field services, voice-first interfaces, etc)
  • A personal interest — a city you want to move to, a hobby, a health question
  • A business idea you've been kicking around
WHAT TO LOOK FOR
Does the engine produce something useful outside its original domain? If not, what broke? The methodology might be too HouseAccount-specific. The extraction schema might have fields that don't apply. This is how you learn which parts of the system are truly general-purpose and which need adapting per domain.
FEED Process a Thought Leader

Find a podcast episode, a YouTube video, or a long blog post from someone whose thinking you respect. Instead of consuming it passively, run it through your engine.

FIND THE SOURCE
Could be a marketplace founder, a home services industry analyst, a tech leader talking about AI in operations, or anyone else whose ideas you'd want to capture. Get the transcript (YouTube auto-generates them; podcast apps often have them; or just paste the URL and let Claude fetch it).
RUN IT THROUGH THE ENGINE
Write a topic file that frames the question: "What operational ideas from [person/episode] apply to HouseAccount?" Point the engine at the transcript. See what comes back — structured insights vs. what you would have absorbed from a casual listen.
THE POINT
Most people listen to a podcast and retain maybe 10% of it. Running it through the engine captures the full signal — and formats it in a way you can reference later, share with your team, or feed into another research run. This is the difference between consuming information and building intelligence.

The shift this week: You've built tools that work with your internal data. Now you have a tool that works with the entire internet. Every interesting thing you encounter is potential input. Start treating information like raw material — something to process, not just consume.
HELP If Something Goes Wrong

"Claude Code isn't following the .md files" — Make sure the CLAUDE.md is in the root of the research engine directory and explicitly references the other files. Claude Code reads CLAUDE.md on launch — if it doesn't know about methodology.md, it won't use it.

"The research output is too shallow" — Your methodology is probably too broad. Narrow the scope: fewer sources, more depth per source. Or add a line to methodology.md saying "for each source, extract at least 5 specific data points" to push for depth over breadth.

"Claude can't access a URL" — Some sites block automated access. Download the content manually (save as PDF, copy the text, grab the transcript) and put it in the research directory. Point Claude at the local file instead.

"The engine works for home services but not other topics" — Your extraction schema is probably too domain-specific. Create a more general version of extraction.md, or create domain-specific extraction files (extraction-home-services.md, extraction-investing.md) and have the topic file specify which one to use.

"I don't know what to research" — Start with the decisions you're making right now at HouseAccount. Every decision has an information gap. "Should we expand to HVAC services?" → research HVAC marketplace demand. "Are we pricing competitively?" → research competitor pricing. The question is the topic file.

Anything else — Drop it in Jottings or bring it to the next session.
